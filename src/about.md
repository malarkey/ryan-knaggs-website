I have perfect pitch, and as a child I often learned music by ear. That process trained my listening and memory early on and gave me a deep connection to how music communicates emotion.

In the early 1980s, my curiosity expanded beyond music into computer programming. As home computers began appearing, I started writing simple video games and exploring how software worked. That interest eventually led to a long career in software engineering. Over time I realised that programming and music share many of the same qualities—structure, logic, creativity, and problem-solving.

A turning point came in 1993 when I experienced the soundtrack to the video game Myst. Its music showed me how powerful audio could be in shaping atmosphere and storytelling within interactive worlds. From that moment on, I became increasingly interested in writing music for visual media.

In 2011, as digital production tools became more accessible, I began composing more seriously. Encouragement from others in the field convinced me to pursue composition professionally and deepen my training.

I hold a Master of Arts in Professional Media Composition for Film, Television, and Video Games from the University of Chichester in collaboration with ThinkSpace Education. In 2024, I began a Master of Fine Arts in Video Game Music Composition and Sound Design at Arts University Bournemouth, focusing further on audio for games and interactive media.
